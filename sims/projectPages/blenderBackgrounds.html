Blender World shaders

As soon as I learned that you could override everything for blender's default scene, I knew I had to make some key personalizations. First and foremost, I wanted an IBL setup. Since most of my concepts take place in an outdoor city environment, I found an urban HDRI on HDRI haven and dumped it into my startup file. I added a world shader that resamples the image and darkens the darkest shadows for a more painterly effect.

I've been using this setup for about 2 years tho, so it seems like it's time for a new one. Since I've also been playing around with some pixel art, a pixel shader seems appropriate. I used an app to take an HDRI from a roof it did a bunch of hard stitching work and shat out some great assets for me to use. Then I do a quick downscale upscale with pixelization in the world shader, reduce the number of colors used (with posterization), and do some curve fiddling to make the colors look nice.

And we can play around with making stuff more or less pixelated.

And that all works fine for a background, but the real magic happens when we zoom in on particular areas and tweak everything to match.

-- Jim McNulty, 2023
